My recommendation

I would tell Replit to stop thinking of this as an Immich replacement.

Instead, think of it as a Digital Asset Manager (DAM)—the kind of software photographers, marketing teams, and businesses use to manage large media libraries.

That means:

Index the NAS into a fast local database.
Generate your own thumbnails and cache them.
Build the browser and dashboard around that database.
Treat the original files on the NAS as the source of truth.

If you ever decide you want AI features in the future, you can plug in an AI service—whether that's Immich, another open-source model, or a cloud API—without redesigning the Media Center. The foundation stays the same, and the AI becomes just another feature instead of the thing everything else depends on.

I think that's a much stronger long-term architecture for the application you're trying to build.
