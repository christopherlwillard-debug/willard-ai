Based on everything we've worked through over the past couple of weeks, I actually think Replit's answer is steering you toward a more complicated solution than you need.

The biggest issue isn't your Z: drive—it's where the code is running.

Right now
Replit Server (Cloud)
        │
        │  ❌ Cannot see your home network
        ▼
Your NAS (Z:)

Even if your NAS has an IP address, Replit's servers still can't just open \\192.168.x.x\Share. That's a private address on your home network.

What I would do instead

Don't build around Replit being connected to your NAS.

Build around the app running locally whenever it needs to touch your files.

When you're home:

Laptop
│
├── Media Center
│
└── Z:\  (NAS)

The app has full access.

Scan
Organize
Generate thumbnails
Optimize
Health checks

Everything works.

Then later...

If you decide you want access from anywhere:

Leave your laptop on (or eventually use a small home PC).

Then use something like:

Tailscale
Cloudflare Tunnel

to reach your app, not your NAS.

Phone

↓

Internet

↓

Home Laptop
(Media Center)

↓

NAS

That architecture scales much better.

There's another option later

If you eventually buy a mini PC, it becomes:

Mini PC
│
├── Media Center
├── Background Tasks
├── Library Engine
└── Z:\ NAS

Then every device connects to the mini PC.

Not to the NAS.

For development

I'd honestly stop worrying about publishing for now.

You're still building major features.

Instead I'd eventually run the app locally on your laptop.

That gives you:

✅ Real Z: drive

✅ Real thumbnails

✅ Real scans

✅ Real performance

Exactly like your future production setup.

I also don't think you need WebDAV or SFTP

Those protocols are useful if you want to access files over the internet, but your Media Center isn't just reading files—it needs to:

scan folders,
detect changes,
generate thumbnails,
compute hashes,
read EXIF,
optimize media,
and maintain an index.

That's much more efficient when the app has direct filesystem access to a mounted drive.

My recommendation

I think your roadmap should be:

Finish building the Media Center in Replit.
When the core features are complete, run it locally on your laptop so it can use Z: directly.
Test it thoroughly against your actual NAS.
Only then think about making it remotely accessible with Tailscale, Cloudflare Tunnel, or a dedicated always-on machine.

That way you're solving one problem at a time, and you avoid spending days building networking infrastructure before the application itself is finished.

From everything we've designed so far, your Media Center is becoming a substantial desktop-class application. I'd focus on getting the software right first, then decide how you want to access it remotely once it's mature.

That's the key question, and the answer depends on where the app is running.

If the app is running in Replit (cloud)

You cannot connect it directly to Z:.

Replit's servers have no access to your Windows drives.

Even if your NAS is mapped as:

Z:\

or

\\MYCLOUD-G1S9ZJ\Wilzard

those only exist on your Windows computer.

If the app is running locally on your laptop

Then it's very easy.

Your app simply stores the path.

For example, in your Settings page:

Library Location

Z:\Photos

or

Z:\

Then your API reads it like any other folder.

Example in Node:

const libraryPath = "Z:\\";

Then:

fs.readdir(libraryPath)

or

fs.stat(...)

or

sharp(...)

Everything works because Windows already mounted Z:.
