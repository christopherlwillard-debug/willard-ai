Overall

I think your implementation order is exactly right:

#144 — Fix the directory cache lifecycle so scans can actually benefit from previous work.
#146 — Keep the thumbnail database and disk in sync permanently, prevent duplicate workers, and support resume.
#145 — Handle interrupted scans cleanly instead of silently resuming them.
#147 — Polish the user experience so the app always feels ready, even while background synchronization is active.

The only thing I'd encourage is to keep the startup health log after development. It may start as a debugging aid, but for a desktop application managing tens of thousands of files on a NAS, it's valuable operational feedback. It gives you immediate visibility into the health of the scanner, cache, thumbnails, and watcher without digging through logs, and it'll make diagnosing future issues much faster.
