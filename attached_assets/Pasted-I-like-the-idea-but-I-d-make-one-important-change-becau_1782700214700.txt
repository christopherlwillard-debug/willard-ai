I like the idea, but I'd make one important change because you're building for very large NAS libraries, not just a few thousand photos.

The goal shouldn't be to generate every thumbnail immediately. If you have 300,000 photos, users shouldn't have to wait hours after every scan before the scan is considered "done."

I'd rewrite the feature like this:

#48 - Intelligent Background Thumbnail Generation

What & Why

After a media scan completes, the Media Center should automatically generate thumbnails for newly indexed media in the background.

Thumbnail generation should never block the completion of a scan. Users can begin browsing their library immediately while thumbnails continue to generate in the background.

The thumbnail engine should prioritize media the user is most likely to view first and avoid regenerating thumbnails that already exist.
