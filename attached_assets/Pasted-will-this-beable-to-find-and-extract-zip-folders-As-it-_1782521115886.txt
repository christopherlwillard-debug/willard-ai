My recommendation

If your ultimate goal is "find every archive on my NAS, extract it safely, organize the contents, send photos to Immich, and index everything else", then I would absolutely expand Version 1 to include Approve → Execute → Verify → Log.

That gives you a working organization tool from day one while still keeping the system safe:

Nothing happens automatically.
Every write operation requires your approval.
Every action is logged.
Every extraction is verified.
The original archive can be preserved unless you explicitly choose otherwise.

I think that's much closer to the Personal Data Operating System you've been envisioning than a permanently read-only design.

From everything we've talked about over the last couple of weeks, your immediate goal is not to build the world's best AI assistant.

I'd change the architecture priorities

Instead of treating these as future enhancements, I'd make them core services.

1. Organization Service (Full Version 1)

This shouldn't be a placeholder anymore.

It should provide:

Analyze a folder
Analyze an archive
Suggest an organization plan
Preview the changes
Extract archives
Verify extracted files
Log every action
Roll back if something fails

Every write operation should require your approval, but the service itself should be fully implemented.

2. Archive Service (Full Version 1)

Not just "peek."

It should support:

Find every ZIP, RAR, 7Z, TAR, GZ, and ISO
Inspect contents
Detect nested archives
Detect password-protected archives
Estimate extraction size
Extract to a chosen destination
Skip duplicate files
Preserve timestamps
Verify extraction completed successfully
3. Organization Engine

This becomes the heart of the cleanup process.

Example workflow:

Instead of only saying:

When an archive contains media:

Continue indexing documents

That way Immich stays responsible for media, and Willard AI orchestrates the process.
