I think you just uncovered another very important clue.

Your screenshot shows:

Database says: 24,551 files
Thumbnail progress says: 24,489 thumbnails
Actual thumbnails on disk: 2,602 files
Thumbnail cache size: only 23 MB

Those numbers don't line up.

That means one of these is happening

Scenario 1 (most likely):
The thumbnail job is not actually completing, even though it reports progress.

It may be:

failing,
restarting,
or repeatedly processing the same records.

Scenario 2:
The progress counter is based on database rows, not actual thumbnail files.

For example:

Generated: 2,603

Remaining: 21,886

…but only 2,602 .webp files physically exist.

That would indicate the database and filesystem are drifting out of sync.

Scenario 3:
The thumbnail job is constantly restarting and never getting past the same point.

You mentioned:

I have left this on for hours and it stays in scanning mode.

That is not normal for a library of ~24k files unless the work is continually being restarted or blocked.

I would not move the cache off the NAS just yet

Since you don't have free SSD space, I wouldn't make that the first priority.

A directory cache JSON is actually quite small (usually KBs to a few MBs), but if your design goal is to keep everything self-contained on the NAS, that's reasonable.

Instead, I'd first answer the question:

Why did the thumbnail engine stop at 2,602?

That seems like the immediate bottleneck.

Here's what I'd ask Replit next

I think we've found another issue. The application reports that it is generating thumbnails for approximately 24,489 files, but the thumbnail cache directory on disk contains only 2,602 files (23 MB). The application has been left running for hours, yet it remains in "Scanning" mode and the thumbnail count does not continue growing.

Please trace the thumbnail pipeline from start to finish and determine:

Why did thumbnail generation stop around 2,602 files?
Is the worker still actively processing files, or is it stalled?
Are the same files being repeatedly queued instead of advancing?
Does the progress bar reflect actual .webp files created, or only database state?
Is the thumbnail job blocked waiting for the scan to complete?
Is there a deadlock or dependency between the scanner and the thumbnail generator?
Is the thumbnail queue continually being rebuilt before it can finish?
Compare:
number of rows with thumbnailPath IS NOT NULL
number of .webp files actually present in cache/thumbnails
number of pending thumbnail jobs
current worker position in the thumbnail queue

Please identify exactly why thumbnail generation appears to stop around 2,600 files instead of progressing toward 24,489.

One thing that also concerns me

The thumbnail folder is only 23 MB.

Even assuming small WebP thumbnails, 2,602 thumbnails averaging around 9 KB each fits that size almost perfectly.

If there were truly 24,000 thumbnails, I'd expect the folder to be much larger—likely hundreds of megabytes depending on your thumbnail dimensions and compression.

So the filesystem appears to support your observation: the thumbnails simply haven't been generated yet, despite what the UI suggests.

That makes me suspect the issue is not "the app keeps regenerating all thumbnails." Instead, it may be that the thumbnail pipeline is stalling after about 2,600 successful outputs, while the UI continues to report that a much larger job is still in progress. That's the next thing I'd investigate before making broader architectural changes.
