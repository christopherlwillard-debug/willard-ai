I actually agree with you, with one important refinement.

The goal of the Optimize Center shouldn't be to ask for approval on every single file. It should be about making good, automatic decisions while never reducing quality.

I'd change the philosophy to this:

Optimize Center = Safe Optimization

The app analyzes every file and classifies it as:

✅ Already optimized
🔄 Can be optimized safely
⚠️ Requires user decision (rare)

For example:

File	Action
HEIC → JPG (highest quality)	Auto-convert
MOV → MP4 (same visual quality)	Auto-convert
JPEG already at 95% quality	Skip
MP4 already H.265 at high quality	Skip
PNG photo that would lose transparency	Ask first
RAW camera file	Never auto-convert

That means most libraries could be optimized with one click.

I think your instincts are right: the Media Center should feel smart, not like it's asking permission every few seconds. If the application can confidently determine that a conversion is safe and won't reduce quality or lose important data, it should just do it.
