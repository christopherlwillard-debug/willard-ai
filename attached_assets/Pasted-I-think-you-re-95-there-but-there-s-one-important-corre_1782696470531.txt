Here's what I'd change
I would remove the assumption that the Replit app is watching the NAS.

Instead, I'd split the architecture into two pieces.

Cloud App (Replit)

↓

Home Agent

↓

NAS

The reason is simple:

Your Replit app cannot directly see your home NAS when it's deployed in the cloud.

Even if today you're only using it at home, separating the "brain" (the app) from the "hands" (the machine that manipulates files) makes the project much easier to evolve later.

The Home Agent can be very small. Today it runs on your laptop when you're home. In the future it could run on a mini PC without changing the Media Center.

One thing I disagree with

This statement:

"Your laptop only runs the app code (~200 MB total)."

That's only true if the app itself is doing file management.

If you also run services like Immich, PostgreSQL, OCR, AI models, or video conversion on the laptop, those services can consume many gigabytes. So it's important to distinguish between the Media Center app and optional background services.

If this were my project, I'd define three layers:
Layer 1 (Storage)
-----------------
NAS
Photos
Videos
Documents
Music

Layer 2 (Media Center)
----------------------
Browse
Search
Organize
Import
Optimize
Backup

Layer 3 (Optional Services)
---------------------------
Immich
OCR
AI
Face Recognition
Video Transcoding

I actually think that's the strongest long-term architecture. It means your Media Center is valuable on its own, and every future service you add—Immich today, something else tomorrow—simply plugs into it rather than becoming something the whole system depends on.
