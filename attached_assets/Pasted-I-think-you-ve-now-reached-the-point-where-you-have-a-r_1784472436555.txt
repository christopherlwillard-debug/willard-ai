Overall

I'd rate the entire roadmap 10/10. The biggest shift is that you're no longer just "making scans faster." You're redesigning the scanner into a streaming, self-tuning synchronization engine.

The one philosophy I'd keep front and center is:

The app should never make the user wait for background work.

If the library opens immediately, thumbnails appear progressively, scans run in the background, and updates stream into the UI, users will perceive the application as fast even when the NAS itself is relatively slow. That perception of responsiveness is often more valuable than shaving another 10–15% off the raw scan time.
